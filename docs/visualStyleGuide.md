# Visual Style Guide
## Sammy and the Brainrot Paradox

This document defines the canonical visual rules for illustrations in *Sammy and the Brainrot Paradox*.  
Its purpose is to ensure visual consistency across all generated images.

All illustration prompts should follow these rules.

---

# Core Visual Style

Illustrations should resemble the aesthetic of a Roblox-style game world.

Key characteristics:

- block-style characters
- simple geometric environments
- bright glowing collectible objects
- clean digital lighting
- vibrant but simple color palettes

Avoid painterly illustration styles.

The world should feel like a **playable game environment**, not a painted scene.

---

# Character Style

Characters should resemble Roblox-style avatars.

Characteristics:

- simple block-like proportions
- expressive but minimal faces
- stylized animation-like poses
- readable silhouettes

Characters should always remain visually clear and recognizable.

---

# Sammy

Age: about 10

Appearance:

- short dark hair
- curious expression
- focused posture when working

Environment scenes may show Sammy at a laptop or computer workstation.

Sammy represents curiosity and experimentation.

---

# Mira

Age: about 10

Appearance:

- observant expression
- calm posture
- often looking at graphs or systems

Mira visually represents analytical thinking.

---

# Dex

Dex appears as a Roblox-style player avatar.

Characteristics:

- energetic posture
- confident movement
- often surrounded by glowing brainrot effects

Dex represents exploration and optimization inside the game.

---

# Brainrot Visual Design

Brainrots are collectible digital artifacts that grant players special powers.

They should appear slightly above the ground and emit glow effects.

Each brainrot must maintain a consistent appearance across all illustrations.

Visual hierarchy:

More powerful brainrots should appear visually more complex, brighter, or larger than common brainrots.

Scale rule:

Brainrots should appear roughly the size of a soccer ball when floating in the game world.

Color hierarchy:

Each brainrot should have a dominant color that clearly distinguishes it:
blue (speed), purple (echo), green (lag), gold (golden), silver (founder), white (observer).

---

## *Velocino Fulminino* — the “speed” brainrot

Appearance:

Blue glowing orb with lightning streaks.

Effect:

Represents extreme movement speed.

---

## *Eccorino Ripetello* — “echo” brainrot

Appearance:

Purple orb surrounded by repeating circular rings.

Effect:

Represents repeated actions.

---

## *Ritardello Staccato* — “lag” brainrot

Appearance:

Green cube with flickering glitch-like edges.

Effect:

Represents brief freezing or delay effects.

---

## *Doratino Amplifino* — “golden” brainrot

Appearance:

Gold sphere with orbiting rings.

Effect:

Amplifies other abilities.

---

## *Fondarino Originello* — “founder” brainrot

Appearance:

Floating silver crown composed of shifting geometric shapes.

It should appear powerful and mysterious.

This brainrot should visually feel more complex and important than the others.

---

## *Osservino Sistemino* — “observer” brainrot

Appearance:

Calm white orb with soft glowing patterns.

It represents understanding rather than raw power.

The glow should feel stable and balanced.

---

# Environment Design

Game environments should resemble Roblox-style maps.

Typical elements include:

- floating platforms
- simple block architecture
- glowing skies
- minimal textures
- clean lighting

Scenes should feel like a playable level inside a game.

---

# Composition Guidelines

Illustrations should focus on **one key narrative moment**.

Each image should clearly show:

- the main character involved
- the brainrot object relevant to the scene
- the action occurring in the moment

Avoid overly crowded compositions.

The main action should always be visually clear.

Camera style:

Illustrations should resemble screenshots from a 3D game camera.
Avoid cinematic film lighting or dramatic painterly perspectives.



---

# Visual Consistency Rules

All illustrations must maintain:

- consistent brainrot shapes
- consistent character proportions
- consistent Roblox-style rendering
- glowing effects for powers

These rules ensure visual continuity throughout the book.
